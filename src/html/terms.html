Cancellation and Non-Attendance Policy

If you are not able to attend your scheduled appointment, we kindly ask that you provide a minimum of 2 hours' notice before your appointment by phoning us. We are happy to rebook your appointment.

If you cancel within the 2 hours or do not attend your scheduled appointment, a non-attendance fee of $40.00 will apply. If you have a cancellation fee, it will require payment before you can schedule another appointment.

Our doctors want to be available for your needs and the needs of all our patients. When a patient does not attend a scheduled appointment, another patient loses the opportunity to be seen. Thank you for being a valued patient and for your cooperation with this policy.

Complaints

If you are unhappy with the care provided, we ask that you speak with your doctor, practice manager, or receptionist today. We are committed to addressing your concerns promptly and effectively.

Patient Information

We are serious about ensuring our patients' health, privacy, and confidentiality. Below are your rights to privacy, your responsibilities, and your Code of Conduct. Please take the time to read it before your next appointment with us.

Your Rights

We believe you have the right to:

  • Respectful care, irrespective of your culture, religious beliefs, ethnicity, or other practices.
  • Adequate information from your doctor to explain your condition and treatment in a clear and understandable language
  • Be advised about the estimated costs of your care.
  • Be involved in making decisions about your healthcare and treatment.

Your Responsibilities

To help us provide you the best care, we encourage you to take your responsibilities seriously:

  • Provide us with accurate and complete information about your health, medications, past medical conditions, etc.
  • Inform us of any new changes in your health condition.
  • Let your doctor or nurse know if you do not understand any explanation or what is expected of you.
  • Follow the recommendations provided by your doctor or nurse.
  • Attend your appointments and let us know in advance if you cannot make the appointment.

Patient Code of Conduct

Under no circumstances do we allow aggressive or violent behaviours toward our patients or our workers. These behaviours include:

  • Threats or threatening behaviours that suggest harm to another person.
  • Direct physical attacks such as hitting, shoving, kicking, pinching, etc.
  • Aggravated assault with a weapon or dangerous object.
  • Sexual harassment or sexual assault.

Electronic Communication

You can be contacted through electronic means via email and/or return SMS. Please be aware there are risks associated with some methods of electronic communications and that your privacy and confidentiality may be compromised. Our practice adheres to the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), the Privacy Act 1988, Privacy and Data Protection Act 2014 (Vic), and Health Records Act 2001 (Vic).
